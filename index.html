We Are PCI

Penson & Company Inc. is an equity investment and holding firm corporation with subsidiaries that spans over five decades of experience in infrastructure development to include 30% of seaports in the country, airports and toll roads.

PCI is the proponent of the Seven Rivers Bulk Water Project in Quezon Provinces, the Metro Manila Flood Mitigation Project and the Sangley Point General Aviation Redevelopment Project. P&CI Engaged in PPP infrastructure development related to renewable energy, bulk water, defense and transport (equity partner of Ausphil Tollways Corp, Defense Resources Inc and Philco Aero Inc), as private proponent or sponsor. Private sponsor of the National Greening Movement Foundation (NGMF) and the Consumer Protection Advocacy Group (CPAG) as components of its CSR programs.

NORTH LUZON EAST EXPRESSWAY

Client's Name

The North Luzon East Expressway is a 6 to 4-lane, 456-kilometer, limited-access toll expressway which involves the application of the new concept or technology called the "Water Sensitive Urban Tollway," in the construction of the Stage 1 of the project from Quezon City to Norzagaray, Bulacan. The subsequent stages stretch up to Gapan, Nueva Ecija on the island of Luzon in the Philippines. The construction involves 3 stages and will be the country's longest tollway when the expressway becomes fully operational.

The first leg of the expressway begins from Commonwealth Avenue in Barangay Balara, Quezon City at the place called "La Mesa Parkways" which will be developed by Ausphil Tollways Corporation. Stage 1 will be a 6-lane tollway beginning from the "La Mesa Parkways” to Norzagaray, Bulacan. Stage 2 will be a 6-lane tollway from Norzagaray, Bulacan to Gapan, Nueva Ecija. The entire cost of this segment is ₱7.8 billion ($190,243,902.44).

The new concept or technology called the "Water Sensitive Urban Tollway," will include the construction of wetlands and roadway system that will integrate the drainage of rainwater from the tollways to the collection and swale system filtration, which is compliant with the 1971 RAMSAR Convention on Wetlands and Philippine Environmental laws. The process involves the collection of rainwater in patented structures to be introduced within the tollways and the filtration of the collected rainwater utilizing wetland plan enzymes to remove pollutants from the swale filtered water. The integration of the wetlands and roadways in the project shall have favorable impact on the environment while producing 10.2 million cubic meters to the La Mesa Reservoir.
